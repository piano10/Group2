Evaluation Methodology

DECIDE Framework

A structured approach to usability evaluation of the MedCare Patient Portal, ensuring user-centered design improvements.

D Determine Goals E Explore Questions C Choose Methods I Heuristic Eval D Decide Ethics E Evaluate & Analyze
D

Determine the Goals

Defining the purpose and scope of evaluation

Introduction

Usability testing for the improvement and optimization of the MedCare System, ensuring it remains an efficient and reliable tool for all users — regardless of technical proficiency.

Evaluation Goals
  • Examine the usability of MedCare from the perspective of patients, buyers, and users — both those who have used the system and those who haven't.
  • Evaluate the effectiveness of content presentation, specifically focusing on clarity of medical information and the purchasing workflow.
  • Focus on first-glance navigation and accessibility of pharmacy services.
E

Explore the Questions

Formulating questions to guide the evaluation

📋 Background Questions (Part II – Demographic Profile)

  • What is the participant's age, gender, course/program, and year level?
  • Does the participant have prior experience with a similar system?
  • How frequently does the participant use technology — daily, weekly, or never?

🎯 Usability & Open-Ended Questions (Part III & IV – SUS)

  • I think that I would like to use this system frequently.
  • I found the system unnecessarily complex.
  • I thought the system was easy to use.
  • I think that I would need the support of a technical person to use this system.
  • I found the various functions in this system were well integrated
  • I thought there was too much inconsistency in this system.
  • I would imagine that most people would learn to use this system very quickly.
  • I found the system very cumbersome to use.
  • I felt very confident using the system.
  • I needed to learn a lot of things before I could get going with this system.
  • What did you like about the system?
  • What improvements can you suggest?
C

Choose Evaluation Approach & Methods

Planning how the evaluation will be conducted

Prospective users were interviewed and observed at Partido State University, specifically students from the BS Accountancy program. Each participant performed real tasks using the MedCare prototype while evaluators recorded time, errors, and verbal feedback using a think-aloud protocol.

Methods Used

🧪 Moderated Usability Testing

In-person sessions where participants performed defined tasks while evaluators observed and noted issues without interference.

📊 System Usability Scale (SUS)

A 10-item post-task questionnaire administered after each session to measure perceived usability on a standardized scale.

D

Decide on the Ethical Issues

Informed consent and participant rights

All participants reviewed and signed an informed consent form prior to participating. The form outlined the study purpose, voluntary nature of participation, and how collected data would be handled confidentially.

Informed Consent Form

This study aims to evaluate the usability of the MedCare prototype system — developed to address healthcare barriers in rural areas, specifically difficulty accessing prescribed medicines.


You are invited to interact with the prototype and perform tasks such as ordering medicine, tracking orders, and checking pharmacy inventory. Afterward, you will complete a 10-item SUS questionnaire. The session takes approximately 10–15 minutes.


Participation is voluntary. You may withdraw at any time. All data is kept confidential and used solely to improve the system.
