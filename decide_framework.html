D - Determine the goals

Introduction

  • Usability Testing for the improvement and optimization of MedCare System, to ensure that MedCare remains an efficient and reliable tool for all users, regardless of their technical proficiency.

GOAL

  • To examine the usability of the Medcare from the perspective of patients, buyers and users who used and never used the system.
  • To evaluate effectiveness of content presentation, specifically focusing on clarity of medical information and purchasing.
  • Focus on: First glance navigation, accessibility pf pharmacy services.

E - Explore the questions

We have to ask the basic information of the prospective users... including:

  • Have the users ever used the online pharmacy application before?
  • What type of pharmacy services do the users frequently require?
  • Which advice do the users typically to for health-related tasks?
  • Do the users know that MedCare provides real-time tracking for pharmacy deliveries?

Then, we will ask the questions that related to using of our application:

  • Can the prospective users find the medicine they need in 3 clicks?
  • Can the prospective users successfully upload a digital prescription to the pharmacy.?
  • Can the prospective users identify the status of their current medecine order?

C - Choose the evaluation approach and methods

In the part of the evaluation approach, we had interview prospective users in Partido State University, specifically the students from BS Accountancy.

D - Decide to the ethical issues

Consent Form

This study aims to evaluate the usability of the MedCare prototype system. The system was developed to address the healthcare barriers faced by the residents in rural areas, specifically the difficulty of finding the prescribe medicine in pharmacies. You are invited to participate in this activity because your feedback as a potential user is essential for ensuring the system is accessible, effective and user-friendly. During this session you will be asked to interact or try using the prototype to perform few tasks such as buying/ordering medicine, tracking orders, and checking pharmacy inventory or pharmacy management. After that you will complete a 10 items system usability scale questionnaire. It will take 10-15 minutes for you to participate in this evaluation. Participation in this usability evaluation is voluntarily, you may choose to withdraw from the activity at any time without any penalty. All the data that gathered during this session will be kept confidential; your identity will not be disclosed, and the information will be used only for the purpose of improving the system. While there are no significant risks involved beyond the time required for participation, your input will help and provide benefits in enhancing healthcare delivery specifically those communities and areas that are distant from the central area of their locality
