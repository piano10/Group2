Participant Background

Meet the six participants who took part in the MedCare usability study — their backgrounds, experience levels, and profiles that shaped our evaluation.

Our usability study involved six participants from the Partido State University community. By splitting the group between those familiar with digital reservations and those completely new to them, we gained a 360-degree view of the MedCare experience. All sessions were recorded on-site to capture authentic user behavior and environmental context.

Selection Criteria

Why these participants were chosen

Inclusion Requirements
  • Currently enrolled students at Partido State University — BS Accountancy program.
  • Diverse mix of tech experience levels: from first-time app users to regular digital shoppers.
  • Range of year levels (1st–4th year) to capture varying maturity in academic and digital exposure.
  • Balanced gender representation to account for any differences in interface perception.
  • At least one participant with prior experience using a reservation or pharmacy platform.
  • Willingness to participate voluntarily and complete the full session including the SUS questionnaire.
Study Setting

All sessions were conducted on-site at Partido State University. Participants interacted with the MedCare prototype on a device provided by the evaluation team while evaluators observed using a think-aloud protocol, recording task times, errors, and qualitative feedback without interference.
