DECIDE Framework Application

This usability evaluation follows the DECIDE framework, a structured approach to evaluation planning and execution for the MedCare portal.

Usability Test Result of Task 1: Book Appointment

Task Success Rate

100% Success Rate (6/6 participants)

All participants successfully completed the appointment booking task. The workflow was intuitive with clear navigation steps.

Time to Complete

Average: 2:45 min
Range: 1:30 - 3:50 min
Most participants completed this task faster than others, indicating good usability.

Satisfaction & Complexity

Satisfaction: 9.2/10
Complexity: Low
Participants found the interface easy to understand and navigate intuitively.

Key Observations

Clear calendar interface, easy date selection, prominent confirmation button. The timeline view helped users visualize appointment slots effectively.

Usability Test Result of Task 2: Review Medical Records

Task Success Rate

67% Success Rate (4/6 participants)

Two participants struggled to locate medical records section. Navigation terminology was confusing to first-time users.

Time to Complete

Average: 4:15 min
Range: 2:20 - 6:10 min
Unsuccessful participants required guidance, extending completion time significantly.

Satisfaction & Complexity

Satisfaction: 6.5/10
Complexity: High
Users found unclear labels and scattered information reduced confidence in finding records.

Pain Points

"Documents," "Results," and "Records" were perceived as three separate sections. Users expected a unified "Health Records" hub.

Usability Test Result of Task 3: Send Message to Provider

Task Success Rate

83% Success Rate (5/6 participants)

One participant confused the messaging interface with notifications. Most participants completed the task successfully with minor hesitation.

Time to Complete

Average: 3:20 min
Range: 2:10 - 5:40 min
Participants navigated to messaging section reasonably well, though some exploration was needed.

Satisfaction & Complexity

Satisfaction: 7.8/10
Complexity: Medium
Decent overall experience but messaging interface could benefit from clearer visual distinction from notifications.

Key Insights

Message compose interface was clear once found. Lack of visual feedback for sent messages caused some uncertainty. Users wanted confirmation that their message was delivered.

Feedback and Suggestions from Participants

Direct feedback collected during think-aloud protocol and post-session interviews.

■

Navigation clarity: "Medical records section should be easier to find. I didn't know where to look for my lab results."

■

Confirmation feedback: "After I booked the appointment, I wasn't sure if it went through. I'd like a clearer success message."

■

Settings discoverability: "I couldn't find where to change my notification settings. The menu is hidden somewhere."

■

Mobile usability: "On my phone, the buttons are too small. I can barely tap them accurately. Need bigger touch targets."

■

Language consistency: "Why is one section called 'Records', another 'Documents', and another 'Results'? It's confusing."

■

Positive feedback: "The appointment booking interface is great. The calendar view made it easy to see available slots."

■

Search feature: "The search function was helpful. I found what I needed quickly instead of clicking through menus."

■

Accessibility: "Good color contrast makes it easy to read. The blue color scheme feels professional and trustworthy."

Summarize Usability Test

From the usability testing conducted on the MedCare portal with six participants, we found that the prototype demonstrates strong performance in specific workflows but has critical areas requiring improvement for broader user adoption. The appointment booking functionality scored exceptionally well (100% success rate, 9.2/10 satisfaction), indicating an intuitive and well-designed core feature. However, medical records retrieval and messaging workflows present challenges, primarily due to unclear terminology and weak visual feedback mechanisms.

Overall, participants demonstrated satisfaction with the system's core functionality and appreciated the clean design, good color contrast, and effective search feature. However, to achieve comprehensive usability across all target users, the following priorities must be addressed: (1) consolidate health information sections under unified terminology, (2) strengthen confirmation feedback for user actions, (3) improve settings and notification discoverability, and (4) enhance mobile responsiveness with larger touch targets. With these improvements implemented, the MedCare portal is well-positioned to provide a user-friendly healthcare experience across diverse user groups and device types.

Recommended Next Steps

High Priority - Implement Immediately

1. Consolidate medical records sections
Merge "Documents," "Results," and "Records" into a single unified "Health Records" hub with consistent labeling and comprehensive search functionality.

2. Add persistent confirmation notifications
Implement toast notifications that remain visible for 5+ seconds with success/error visual indicators and dismiss buttons for user control.

Medium Priority - Plan for Next Release

3. Enhance settings discoverability
Make the settings icon 20% larger and position it consistently in top-right navigation. Add user profile card dropdown and keyboard shortcuts.

4. Improve mobile navigation
Convert sidebar to hamburger menu with clearer label. Ensure buttons meet 48x48px tap-size standards. Test on actual mobile devices.

Low Priority - Future Enhancements

5. Visual polish & onboarding
Enhance appointment calendar styling. Add helpful onboarding tips for first-time users. Consider tutorial overlays for complex workflows.
