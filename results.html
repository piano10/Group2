Usability Results

MedCare
Patient Portal

A comprehensive SUS-based usability evaluation conducted with 40 participants across multiple programs at Partido State University.

DECIDE Framework Application

The DECIDE framework was applied as the structured methodology for this usability evaluation of the MedCare Patient Portal. D — We determined the goals: to assess first-glance usability, navigation clarity, and overall user satisfaction. E — We explored background and task-based questions covering prescription tracking, delivery monitoring, and key portal interactions. C — We chose moderated usability testing combined with the System Usability Scale (SUS) questionnaire as our primary methods. I — We identified practical issues including participant recruitment, logistics, and scheduling across programs. D — We decided on ethical issues by securing informed consent forms signed voluntarily by all participants, ensuring confidentiality. E — We evaluated and analyzed data against usability targets, computing SUS scores, task metrics, and satisfaction ratings for each participant.

Overall SUS Score

The System Usability Scale (SUS) is a reliable, 10-item questionnaire producing a score from 0–100. Scores above 68 are considered above average usability. The MedCare Patient Portal achieved a mean SUS score of 71.5 out of 100, placing it in the Good usability band.

Usability Test Result — Task 1: Log In & Account Setup

Participants were asked to log into the MedCare Patient Portal using one of three methods: using an existing account (with or without forgotten password), creating a new account, or continuing with Google.

🔐 Task Description

Log in or create an account. Options included: recovering a forgotten password, registering a new account, and OAuth via Google sign-in.

👥 Participants

40 total — 28 Female, 12 Male. Mostly 1st year (28) and 2nd year (10) students across 8 programs.

🔍 Key Observations

Most participants found the Google sign-in option the quickest. Some new users were unsure which option to choose first, indicating a need for clearer onboarding cues.

Usability Test Result — Task 2: Homepage & Shop Navigation

Participants navigated the homepage to explore pharmacy locations, browse and purchase products through the shop, manage the cart, and proceed through the payment flow.

🏠 Subtasks Covered

Check pharmacy locations · Call the pharmacy · Browse & buy products · Proceed to payment · Choose payment method · Manage cart (delete / adjust quantity).

📊 SUS Insight

Q3 (I found the system easy to use) had the highest average rating of 4.49 / 5, indicating strong ease-of-use perception during navigation tasks.

⚠️ Pain Points

Q2 (unnecessary complexity) averaged 2.90 and Q10 (need to learn things) averaged 2.95, pointing to a moderate learning curve in the shop flow.

Usability Test Result — Task 3: Messaging, Notifications & Profile

Participants used the messaging feature to converse with the pharmacy, checked notifications to track order status, and managed their profile — including editing info, changing language, managing payment methods, and logging out.

💬 Messaging & Notifications

View messages · Open pharmacy conversation · Send a message · Check notifications · Track order status and progress.

👤 Profile Management

Edit profile information · Change language settings · Manage payment methods · Log out of account.

💡 Key Insights

Q6 (inconsistency) and Q8 (cumbersome to use) had the lowest negative-item averages (2.45 and 2.38), showing the interface is perceived as consistent and relatively fluid in profile interactions.

Feedback and Suggestions from Participants

Feedback was gathered through the SUS questionnaire responses and direct observations during moderated testing sessions. The following themes emerged from the data.

✅

High Ease of Use
Q3 — "I found the system easy to use" — achieved the highest average rating of 4.49 / 5, with 20 participants selecting Strongly Agree. Most users could navigate core features intuitively after brief exploration.

⚠️

Desire for Simpler Navigation
Q2 — "I found the system unnecessarily complex" — averaged 2.90 with 7 participants Strongly Disagreeing but 8 Strongly Agreeing. Some users felt the multi-step shop and checkout flow added unnecessary steps.

📱

Perceived Integration
Q7 — "I would imagine most people would learn quickly" — averaged 4.36 / 5. 18 participants Agreed and 18 Strongly Agreed, suggesting confidence that new users can onboard without much guidance.

🔧

Need for Technical Support Concern
Q4 — "I would need technical support" — averaged 2.52, with 8 participants in the Strongly Disagree camp but 2 Strongly Agreeing. A subset of less tech-savvy users (7 who reported "Never" using tech) expressed higher need for assistance.

🌐

Program-Specific Variation
BSA participants scored notably higher on average (mean ~74.5), while BACOM participants showed more variability (range 50–57.5), suggesting the portal's design may align better with students already experienced in administrative or data-heavy tasks.

Summarize Usability Test

The MedCare Patient Portal usability evaluation involved 40 participants from Partido State University across 8 programs (BSED, BSED Math, BSED ValEd, BSED SS, BSBA FM, BSA, BACOM, BSBIO), predominantly 1st-year students (70%) with 65% being daily technology users. The DECIDE framework guided the evaluation, employing moderated usability testing and the System Usability Scale (SUS) as primary instruments.

The portal achieved a mean SUS score of 71.5 (median 72.5), which places it above the industry benchmark of 68 and in the "Good" usability classification. Positive-statement items (Q1, Q3, Q5, Q7, Q9) consistently scored between 4.22–4.49, reflecting that users found the system easy to use, well-integrated, and learnable. Negative-statement items (Q2, Q4, Q6, Q8, Q10) averaged between 2.38–2.95, indicating that most users did not find the system unnecessarily complex, cumbersome, or inconsistent — though moderate room for improvement exists, particularly around the complexity of the shop/checkout workflow and onboarding for first-time users with low prior tech exposure.

Recommended Next Steps

🔴 High Priority — Implement Immediately

Simplify Shop & Checkout Flow
Q2 and Q10 flagged unnecessary complexity and a learning curve in the shop. Reduce checkout steps, add progress indicators, and provide clear cart management controls to lower cognitive load.

Onboarding Guidance for New Users
7 participants who reported "Never" using similar tech scored lower (avg ~60). Implement a brief guided tour or contextual tooltips triggered on first login to ease initial navigation.

🟡 Medium Priority — Plan for Next Release

Improve Navigation Labels & Consistency
Feedback from findings.html confirmed unclear navigation labels. Standardize terminology across all sections and ensure menu placement is predictable on both mobile and desktop.

Enhance System Feedback & Confirmations
Add success/error confirmation messages for key actions (sending messages, completing purchases, updating profile), addressing the "Lack of Feedback" finding identified during testing.

🟢 Low Priority — Future Enhancements

Expand Mobile Responsiveness
Several elements did not scale properly on smaller screens. Conduct dedicated mobile usability testing and refine layouts for sub-400px viewports to improve readability and interaction targets.

Expand Evaluation Scope
With only 40 participants (mostly 1st–2nd year BSEd/BSA/BACOM), future rounds should include older users, healthcare staff, and non-student demographics to improve external validity of findings.
